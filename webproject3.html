Treaties


Treaty 1

What are the historic terms of the Treaty, meaning what was included in the Treaty 1 (ex. Outside promises, how much land each family of five were to receive, etc.)?


Treaty 1, signed in 1871, gave 160 acres of land to each family of five, $3 per person every year, promised schools, banned alcohol on reserves, and included extra promises like clothing and farming tools for chiefs, added in 1875. The “outside promises” were acknowledged by the government in 1875 after Indigenous leaders insisted that verbal promises made during negotiations—like clothing for chiefs and farming tools—were just as important as what was written in the treaty.

What makes this Treaty 1 different from other Treaties in Canada.


Treaty 1 is different because it was the first Numbered Treaty (signed in 1871), included later “outside promises” like farming tools and clothing not in the written text, and was understood by Indigenous peoples as a land-sharing agreement, while the Crown saw it as land surrender

How is the Treaty 1 implemented today?

Treaty 1 is implemented today through things like annual payments, development of Indigenous-run lands like Naawi-Oodena , and efforts to honor treaty rights through education, land use, and reconciliation projects
